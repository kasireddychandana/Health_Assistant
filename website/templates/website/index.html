Diseases

Diseases are abnormal conditions that affect the body or mind, causing discomfort, dysfunction, or harm. They can result from infections, genetic factors, environmental influences, or lifestyle choices.

Infectious Diseases

Caused by pathogens (bacteria, viruses, fungi, parasites).

Example: COVID-19, Tuberculosis, Malaria, Influenza.

Non-Communicable Diseases

Not spread from person to person. Often linked to genetics or lifestyle.

Example: Diabetes, Cancer, Heart Disease, Hypertension.

Neuroligical Diseases

Affect the brain, spinal cord, or nerves.

Example: Parkinson’s Disease, Alzheimer’s, Epilepsy

Genetic Diseases

Inherited from parents due to DNA mutations.

Example: Sickle Cell Anemia, Cystic Fibrosis, Hemophilia.

Mental Health Diseases

Affect thoughts, emotions, and behavior.

Example: Depression, Anxiety, Schizophrenia.

Deficiency Diseases

Caused by lack of essential nutrients.

Example: Rickets (Vitamin D deficiency), Scurvy (Vitamin C deficiency).

Precautions

Precautions refer to preventive measures taken to avoid harm, reduce risks, or protect against diseases. They help ensure safety and well-being.

Infectious Diseases

Infectious diseases are caused by pathogens like bacteria, viruses, fungi, or parasites. They can spread through direct contact, air, water, food, or insect bites.

Non-Communicable Diseases

Non-communicable diseases (NCDs) like diabetes, heart disease, cancer, and chronic respiratory diseases can be prevented or managed with a healthy lifestyle.

Genetic Diseases

Genetic diseases are inherited conditions caused by mutations in genes. While they cannot always be prevented, certain precautions can help manage risks and improve quality of life.

Neurological Diseases

Neurological diseases affect the brain, spinal cord, and nerves, including conditions like Alzheimer’s, Parkinson’s, epilepsy, multiple sclerosis, and stroke

Mental Health Disorders

Mental health disorders, such as depression, anxiety, bipolar disorder, and schizophrenia, can be managed and, in some cases, prevented with proper precautions.

Deficiency Diseases

Deficiency diseases occur due to a lack of essential nutrients such as vitamins, minerals, or proteins.
